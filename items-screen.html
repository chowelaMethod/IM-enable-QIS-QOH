Inventory Management (IM)

Track stock levels and manage orders with inventory synced across locations for sales orders, purchase orders, and items.

Inventory management status
New transactions become inventory transactions and stop syncing to QuickBooks. When disabled, new transactions become accounting-only; previously synced transactions are unaffected.

Enable Inventory Management

The new Inventory management feature tracks your quantities across in Stock, Committed, Expected, and Available statuses.

To start tracking your current items, assign them to locations using stock adjustments or purchase orders.

Important information

  • Transactions created before today, and any transactions synced from QuickBooks, won't get the inventory management features.
  • New Sales Orders and Purchase Orders created in Method will be tracked as inventory transactions and will not sync to QuickBooks. They may also be blocked if stock is insufficient.
  • If your inventory screens are customized, contact a solution specialist to create an integration plan before enabling. Disabling later requires restoring those screens to default by removing screen redirects. Not sure how? Contact support
Disable Inventory Management

Disabling Inventory Management will return your account to an accounting-only stock state.

Existing inventory transactions will remain visible in read-only mode. You won't be able to edit inventory activity until Inventory Management is re-enabled.

Important information

  • All inventory screens, quantities, and fulfillment features will be hidden
  • Any Sales Orders or Purchase Orders created will permanently remain accounting-only records. These transactions will never be added to inventory tracking, even if you re-enable later.
  • If you re-enable Inventory Management later, inventory counts won't include activity from the disabled period. You'll need to perform stock adjustments to realign quantities.
Enable Inventory Management (IM)

The new Inventory Management feature tracks your item quantities across one or more locations, and ties stock levels directly to your sales orders and purchase orders.

Once enabled, new transactions will become inventory transactions and will no longer sync quantity changes back to QuickBooks automatically.

Important information

  • Transactions created before today are unaffected and will continue to sync normally.
  • New Sales Orders and Purchase Orders will update Method's stock levels directly.
  • If your item screens are customized, some fields may need to be repositioned. Contact support for help.
  • Inventory Management can be disabled later from this page. Historical inventory data is preserved.
